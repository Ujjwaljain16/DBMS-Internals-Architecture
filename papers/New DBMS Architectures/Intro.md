# *New DBMS Architectures* (Stonebraker, Readings in Database Systems)

This reading explains how modern database systems moved away from the traditional **“one-size-fits-all” row-store architecture** that dominated databases until the early 2000s. It highlights the major architectural shifts that reshaped database design and performance.

The paper argues that different workloads require **specialized database architectures**, and describes four major transformations in the DBMS landscape.
